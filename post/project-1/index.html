Project I: Cyclistic Bikeshare Trip Data Project (Python)

May 30, 2022

In this project, I analyzed the monthly trip data of bikeshare company from May 2021 to April 2022. My analysis focused on identifying the yearly trend and monthly changes, as well as how casual riders and member riders’ riding behaviors differ.

Language & Tools: Python(packages: pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn)

Skills: data wrangling, data analysis, data visualization

GitHub Link: Bikeshare_trip_data_Python_project

Viz:

Discoveries:

  • Warmer months, May through October, are the popular months for bike riding, while colder months, November through April, are much slower;
  • Casual riders show greater variability in terms of the amount of monthly riders made in a year. In other words, their riding behavior are more sensitive to weather and time of year.
  • On a weekly basis, both casual riders and annual members ride more on Saturday and Sunday.
  • The popular riding stations are identified, such as Streeter Dr & Grand Ave. 5. In terms of bike types, both types of riders prefer electric bikes and classic bikes over docked bikes.
  • In terms of duration of each ride, casual riders tend to consistently spend significantly longer time on each ride than member riders.
